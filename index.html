icondesign

sustainability affects all of us

Study project in the second semester:

The assignment was to design two icon sets for two different target groups (freely selectable). Each set consists of 7 icons and is intended for a "sustainability app". We had to make sure that the icons were appropriate for the target groups, the context and that they were uniform and consistent. Furthermore, it should be ensured that the appropriate metaphors have been found for each word, making the icons understandable. It is also important that the icons are visually appealing and aesthetic.

The first icon set is aimed at younger users. The central element is the bee. It is not only important for the preservation of all plant and animal species, but also a symbol for environmental protection.
The second icon set is aimed at adult users. The icons are deliberately reduced to continuous lines to reflect the developments and progress of today's environmental movement.

For creating the icons, I used Adobe Illustrator.

screendesign

The task was to redesign the THI timetable to make it reflect the university´s character more. The new timetable was supposed to include the housecolors, the logo and also use the THI font.

Data visualization

Built on sand

Visualizing Data about sustainability, climate protection and environment – a topic as current as never before.

My decision to choose „sand“ as my subject was rooted in my upbringing in the sandy region „Sand am Main“. Sand is one of the most underestimated resources, which in comparison to fading resources such as oil does not get a lot of attention. Although most industrial branches rely heavily on sand.
My poster is supposed to show viewers the dimensions in which sand is used and the outcomes of ist increasing mining.

The poster was part of the „Klimawandelweg“ in Altomünster and was also shown at the THI.

app: Lexicare

because caring takes knowledge

Design of the human-machine interface

How can we help older adults 65+ in their well-being and wellness through interfaces that respond to tangible and embodied interactions?
This is the question we were asked to grapple with as a team of two in the 3rd semester and create an app with accompanying video to explain the app and concept.

We chose "Lexicare."

Lexicare helps keep track of all the important forms and steps to follow when caring for someone. So our target group is not the elderly themselves, but the family caregivers. Nevertheless, it affects them indirectly and improves their well-being. We wanted to give them a tool to reduce the stress that caregiving can cause.

The project included ideation, sketching, prototyping, interface design, filming and editing the video. We used Adobe Premiere Pro, Adobe After Effects, Adobe Illustrator, and Adobe XD.
